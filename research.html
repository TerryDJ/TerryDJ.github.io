Research

I build microfluidic tools that preserve small samples, simplify workflows, and make high-content biological assays more accessible.

Sample-Sparing Diagnostics

My diagnostic work focuses on reducing sample and reagent loss in workflows where every microliter matters. This includes under-oil sample processing, magnetic-bead extraction, colorimetric isothermal amplification, and device formats that can be run with common lab equipment.

Whole-Blood Immune Assays

I led development of micro blood analysis technology, a platform for multiplexed neutrophil phenotyping and functional analysis from microliter whole-blood samples. The system supports phagocytosis, neutrophil extracellular trap analysis, cytokine profiling, optical metabolic imaging, and gene-expression workflows.

Open Microfluidics and Spatial Biology

I co-developed platforms for reconfigurable biochemical gradients and long-duration cell culture. These systems support cancer organoid culture, mesenchymal stem cell co-culture, astrocyte response studies, immune-cell migration, and downstream high-content imaging or omics workflows.

Prototyping and Translation

Across research roles, I have worked from concept through device fabrication, assay optimization, data analysis, documentation, and transfer-ready protocols. My fabrication experience includes SU-8 and PDMS soft lithography, CNC micromachining, 3D printing, surface patterning, portable fixtures, and microscope-integrated instrumentation.
