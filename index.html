Microfluidic platforms for diagnostics and biology from microliter samples.

I am a biomedical engineer and microfluidics scientist with 7+ years of experience designing low-volume fluidic systems, immune assays, nucleic-acid workflows, and prototype diagnostic devices.

My work centers on making complex biological measurements easier to run from limited clinical samples. During my Ph.D. in the Beebe Lab at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, I led and co-developed open and under-oil microfluidic platforms for whole-blood immune-cell analysis, RNA extraction, spatial biology, and point-of-care infectious disease diagnostics. I later supported product-oriented diagnostic R&D at Salus Discovery, LLC.

Current focus: microfluidic consumables, sample preparation, assay integration, and instrument-light workflows for diagnostics, cell assays, and translational biology.

Selected Highlights

  • Led development of micro blood analysis technology, a multiplexed neutrophil assay platform from less than 4 uL of whole blood.
  • Co-developed OIL-TAS, an under-oil platform for integrated RNA extraction and SARS-CoV-2 detection.
  • Co-developed open microfluidic platforms for reconfigurable gradients, spatial biology, and long-duration cell-culture assays.
  • Co-inventor on multiple microfluidic and sample-extraction patent filings.
  • Mentored 6+ junior researchers and helped standardize protocols for technology transfer.
